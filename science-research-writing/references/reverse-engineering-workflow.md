# Reverse-Engineering Workflow

Use this method as the controlling workflow for STEMM research writing.

## Intake

Collect or infer:

- genre and target section
- target journal or comparable venue
- reader scope: microcommunity, broad field, interdisciplinary, applied, clinical,
  industry, or policy
- study achievement, contribution, impact, and boundary
- available evidence: figures, tables, methods, comparisons, statistics, datasets
- target articles: ideally at least four recent, well-written papers from the
  target journal or close field

If target articles are absent, say that the model is provisional and use generic
models only.

## Target Article Criteria

Prefer articles that are:

- recent, normally within five years
- from journals the user wants to publish in
- close to the user's topic and article type
- clearly structured into recognizable sections
- successful and well written
- short enough to analyze efficiently

## Four-Step Method

1. Build a sentence- or paragraph-based model.
   Label what each unit does: establishes importance, maps literature, identifies
   a gap, states method, reports result, interprets, limits, or claims value.

2. Mine language for those functions.
   Capture reusable words and phrase families only after identifying the function.
   Do not collect decorative synonyms.

3. Identify grammar and writing choices that communicate meaning.
   Check tense, active/passive, subject choice, articles, prepositions, modal
   verbs, sentence starts, paragraph entries, and citation placement.

4. Continually update the model.
   Writing conventions change across fields, journals, online article formats,
   and time. Current target examples override a generic model when the evidence
   is clear.

## Planning Scaffold

Before drafting, answer:

- What is the text?
- Who might read it now and later?
- What can readers be assumed to know?
- What section divisions and subtitles are normal?
- Where should figures and tables appear so they support the narrative?
- What is each paragraph's function?
- What is each sentence's function?
- What is the magnetic destination point of the paper?

## Drafting Rule

Write from function to language:

`function -> evidence -> reader need -> sentence/paragraph -> language check`

Do not begin from a polished sentence bank. Phrases are only useful after the
role of the sentence is known.

## Audit Rule

When reviewing existing text, diagnose in this order:

1. wrong genre or reader assumptions
2. missing achievement/contribution/impact
3. missing or misplaced section functions
4. claim without evidence or evidence without claim
5. unclear boundary or certainty level
6. paragraph and sentence flow
7. grammar and vocabulary risks
