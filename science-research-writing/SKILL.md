# Science Research Writing

## Core Rule

Follow Hilary Glasman-Deal's reverse-engineering method before writing or
auditing. Do not treat the task as sentence polishing until the section job,
reader, target genre, target articles, and claim-evidence-boundary logic are
clear.

If the user provides no target articles, ask for them when the output depends on
journal convention. If the user needs momentum, proceed with the generic models
in `references/section-models.md`, explicitly mark the result as a provisional
model, and list what target articles are needed to localize it.

## Mandatory Workflow

1. Identify genre and reader: research article, review, thesis-paper chapter,
   conference abstract, report, letter, or other STEMM text.
2. Identify the target section: title, abstract, introduction, methods, results,
   discussion, conclusion, full outline, or whole-paper audit.
3. Establish the destination point: achievement, contribution, impact, and
   boundary of the study.
4. Reverse engineer current target examples when available:
   - sentence or paragraph function
   - order of functions
   - language used to communicate each function
   - grammar choices that carry meaning
5. Build or choose a section model before drafting.
6. Draft or revise from function to language, not from language to decoration.
7. Audit with claim-evidence-boundary and language-risk checks.

Never invent data, mechanisms, references, novelty, journal norms, sample sizes,
statistics, limitations, or target-article patterns.

## Operating Standards

- Make the reader know what information is doing, not only what it says.
- Prefer explicit narrative links over bare data, loose facts, or unexplained
  numbers.
- Use repetition for stable concepts. Do not vary terminology with thesaurus
  substitutions.
- Treat grammar as meaning: tense, article choice, passive voice, subject choice,
  prepositions, modal verbs, and citation placement can change the claim.
- Use risk-reducing language when interpretation outruns direct evidence.
- Preserve ambiguity only if the science is genuinely ambiguous and the prose
  says so.
- In every title and abstract, ensure that the promised contribution is fulfilled
  by the paper's evidence and conclusion.

## Default Output

For drafting:

1. `Model used:` section model or target-article-derived model.
2. `Draft:` polished English unless the user asks otherwise.
3. `Function map:` paragraph or sentence jobs.
4. `Claim-evidence-boundary map:` supported, under-supported, or missing.
5. `Target-article gaps:` what still needs localization.

For auditing:

1. `Major risks:` ordered by likely publication/reviewer impact.
2. `Reverse-engineering diagnosis:` missing, misplaced, or overloaded functions.
3. `Line/paragraph edits:` revised text or precise edit instructions.
4. `Language-risk checks:` ambiguity, tense, certainty, reference, density.
5. `Next actions:` minimal steps to repair the manuscript.

For Chinese input, provide polished English first, then concise Chinese notes on
logic, missing evidence, and why the structure changed.
