# Function-Anchored Phrase Bank

Use only after the function of a sentence or paragraph is fixed. These are
language families for known communicative functions, not synonyms for varying
technical terms. Keep technical terms stable; never thesaurus-hop. Always
calibrate against target articles — these are starting points, not a house style.

## How to use

1. Decide the function first (see `section-models.md`).
2. Pick a phrase family that carries that function.
3. Check tense, certainty, and ownership against the claim.
4. Replace generic nouns with your real subject; delete any phrase that adds no
   function.

## Introduction

- Establish importance: *X plays a central role in…*, *X has become increasingly important for…*, *Understanding X is critical to…*
- Background fact (present simple): *X is defined as…*, *X consists of…*
- Map prior work (present perfect / past): *Previous studies have shown that…*, *Several authors have reported…*, *X et al. (2021) demonstrated…*
- Identify a gap: *However, little is known about…*, *X remains poorly understood*, *Few studies have addressed…*, *It is still unclear whether…*, *No previous work has…*
- State the present work: *Here we…*, *In this study, we…*, *This paper presents…*
- Aim / contribution: *The aim of this study is to…*, *We show that…*, *Our main contribution is…*

## Methods

- Overview / purpose (infinitive): *To [aim], we…*, *The procedure consisted of three stages.*
- Materials and source: *X was obtained from [supplier]*, *We used X (n = …).*
- Procedure (past simple, often passive): *X was measured using…*, *Samples were prepared by…*, *Data were analysed with…*
- Justify a choice: *X was chosen because…*, *Following [ref], we…*, *To avoid X, we…*
- Note a constraint without over-apology: *Owing to X, …*, *X was limited to…*

## Results

- Point to a graphic (present simple): *Figure 1 shows…*, *As shown in Table 2,…*, *Figure 3 presents…*
- General statement: *Overall, …*, *In general, X increased with Y.*
- Specific result (past simple) + meaning: *X increased by 20% (Fig. 2), indicating…*, *A significant difference was observed between…*
- Compare: *Consistent with [ref], …*, *In contrast to…*, *This agrees with…*
- "So what?" comments: *suggesting that…*, *indicating that…*, *which implies…*

## Discussion / Conclusion

- State achievement: *This study shows that…*, *We have demonstrated…*
- Interpret with hedging: *These results suggest that…*, *may be attributed to…*, *One possible explanation is…*, *could reflect…*
- Map to literature: *in agreement with…*, *This contrasts with previous reports that…*
- Limit the claim: *A limitation of this study is…*, *These findings should be interpreted with caution because…*
- Future / application: *Future work could…*, *These findings may enable…*

## Certainty ladder

Match the verb to the evidence; do not climb it to sound confident or descend it
to hedge a result the data fully support.

`is / shows` (durable, well supported)
→ `demonstrates / confirms` (strong, direct)
→ `indicates / shows that` (supported)
→ `suggests / is consistent with` (interpretive)
→ `may / might / could / raises the possibility that` (beyond direct evidence)

## Checks

- Every phrase serves a function already identified — not decoration.
- Technical terms stay constant; only connective and framing language varies.
- The certainty word matches the evidence (see ladder).
- The phrase family matches what target articles in the venue actually use.
