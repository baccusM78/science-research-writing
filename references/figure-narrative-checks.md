# Figure–Narrative Checks

Use when the text refers to figures or tables, or when writing or auditing
captions. Goal: the figures and the prose tell one consistent story and refer to
each other correctly. Visual design and rendering belong to a figure tool; this
file covers the writing side.

## Text ↔ figure linkage

- Every figure and table is cited in the text, in numerical order.
- The text says what the reader should *see* and what it *means*, not just "(see Fig. 1)".
- Report the key value and its meaning; do not re-list every number already in the graphic.
- Naked-number check: each reported number is tied to a comparison, baseline, or interpretation.

## Captions

- The caption is self-contained: axes, units, sample size (n), error bars,
  symbols, and abbreviations are all defined.
- A reader who sees only the figure and caption can understand the point.
- Tense: describe what the figure *shows* (present); what was *done* (past).
- The caption states the result or point, not only the variables plotted.

## Consistency

- Numbers, trends, and signs in the text match the figure.
- Units and symbol definitions are identical across text, figure, and caption.
- A finding shown in a figure is *pointed to* in Results (present) and
  *interpreted* in Discussion (hedged) — not interpreted twice, and not left uninterpreted.

## Placement and economy

- Each figure appears where the narrative needs it.
- No figure merely duplicates a table, or vice versa.
- Remove decoration that hides the claim; keep what carries a function.

## Audit order

1. Is every graphic referenced, and in order?
2. Does the text state the meaning, not just the location?
3. Is each caption standalone and correctly tensed?
4. Do text and figure agree on numbers, units, and trend?
5. Is each finding pointed to once in Results and interpreted once in Discussion?
