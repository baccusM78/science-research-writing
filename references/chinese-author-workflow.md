# Chinese Author Workflow

Use when the source is Chinese, mixed Chinese-English, translated prose, or lab
notes.

## Core Stance

Do not translate sentence by sentence until the argument is clear. Rebuild the
research logic first, then write English.

## Intake Questions

Identify:

- target section and journal
- Chinese source type: notes, draft, outline, thesis prose, grant prose, or
  already translated English
- central achievement and contribution
- figure/table evidence
- intended reader
- claim boundary and limitations

## Common Repairs

- Convert background-heavy openings into a gap-led Introduction.
- Turn lists of facts into a narrative route toward the study.
- Make the value of the study explicit rather than implied.
- Replace broad praise words with specific contribution language.
- Split overlong Chinese-style information chains into linked English sentences.
- Avoid synonym variation that destabilizes technical terms.
- State ownership: prior work, this study, our model, the dataset, or the method.
- Calibrate certainty; do not let translation software overstate conclusions.

## Output Pattern

For Chinese input:

1. Provide polished English first.
2. Add concise Chinese notes explaining:
   - section function
   - main structural changes
   - missing evidence or boundary
   - high-risk language choices

If the Chinese source lacks enough scientific evidence, output a scaffold with
placeholders instead of inventing content.
